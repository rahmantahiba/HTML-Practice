Abstract

The research paper will discuss the background and subtypes of schizophrenia which are paranoid, catatonic, residual, disorganized, and undifferentiated. However, since there are many similarities between these types, the two subtypes will be compared and contrasted. It will also include information about how the damage to brain chemistry harms cognition, making them lose awareness of themselves. Schizophrenia is difficult to locate since it has various subtypes since the individuals exhibit many behaviors that make each case unique. There are positive and negative symptoms caused by the lack of motor control and increased parietal cortex activity. The development of the symptoms is caused by the dysfunction of the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex caused in paranoid schizophrenic patients. Undifferentiated schizophrenic patients have decreased function in the temporal-hippocampal, which hinders their memory and attention. It is essential to raise awareness of schizophrenia since it’s difficult to think of proper diagnostic criteria and support for the individuals inflicted with schizophrenia. In order to improve the conditions for the betterment of the patients, it is essential to understand the neurology and the harmful effects of schizophrenia to give them hope for recovery.
